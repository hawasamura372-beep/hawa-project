About FloodWatch SL

Protecting Sierra Leone
from Flood Disaster.

Sierra Leone experiences devastating floods every rainy season. The 2017 Regent mudslide killed over 1,000 people. Without early warning, communities cannot prepare or evacuate in time. FloodWatch SL exists to change that.

Our network of river sensors, combined with community reporting and SMS alerts, provides the earliest possible warning — giving families, local councils, and emergency services the time they need to respond before disaster strikes.

Alert System

Three-Level Warning System

FloodWatch SL uses internationally recognised flood alert levels adapted for Sierra Leone's river systems.

Level 1
🟢 Normal – Safe
Water level: 0 – 1.5m
Rivers are within normal range. No flooding risk. Continue usual activities. Monitoring continues automatically 24/7.
No action needed
Level 2
🟡 Watch – Stay Alert
Water level: 1.5m – 2.5m
Water is rising. Residents in flood-prone zones should prepare emergency bags and monitor updates. Avoid crossing rivers on foot.
Prepare for possible evacuation
Level 3
🔴 Alert – Act Now
Water level: above 2.5m
Critical flooding imminent or underway. Evacuate low-lying areas immediately. Move to higher ground. Call 999 for assistance.
Evacuate immediately
